The device is also capable of running Android apps as well, but only after they have been converted into an iOS compatible format. Additionally, all applications downloaded onto <i>SCP-16995</i> will remain on the device even after power has been removed from the device.<br><br>It is currently unknown how or why this anomaly exists; all attempts at reverse engineering the software modifications have failed. Testing has revealed that all functions of both Android and iOS apps can be used normally on <i>SCP-16995</i> without any issues or glitches being encountered during use.<br><br><h3>Addendum 16995.1 : </h3><br><br>On ██/██/████, personnel attempting to access the device's internal storage discovered a folder containing several documents. Analysis of these documents revealed that <i>SCP-16995</i> was created by an individual known only as <i>"Dr. ███████"</i>. The documents describe the development process of <i>SCP-16995</i> and its purpose; to create a device capable of running both Android and iOS applications without any compatibility issues or glitches.<br><br><h3>Addendum 16995.2 : </h3>Interview with  Dr. ███████<br><br>On ██/██/████, personnel were able to locate and interview Dr. ███████ regarding the development of <i>SCP-16995</i>. During the interview, Dr. ███████ stated that they had developed <i>SCP-16995</i> as a means of creating a device capable of running both Android and iOS applications without any compatibility issues or glitches. When asked why they had chosen to develop such a device, Dr. ███████ responded that it was due to their belief that such a device would be beneficial for people who use both types of operating systems on different devices but still wanted access to all their apps in one place.<br><br><h3>Addendum 16995.3 : </h3>Experiment Log <br><br>Experiment #: 16995-1<br><br><b>Subject: </b><i>SCP-16995</i><br><br><b>Procedure: </b>An Android application was downloaded onto <i>SCP-16995</i> and launched.<br><br><b>Results: </b>The application ran normally, with no glitches or compatibility issues encountered.</div>
