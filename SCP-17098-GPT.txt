<style>.justifier {  text-align: justify;  text-justify: inter-word;}</style><div class='justifier'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-17098 is  a tricky lobster man who likes to steal.</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-17098<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Keter<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-17098</i> is a humanoid entity of unknown origin. It is approximately 2 meters tall and appears to be composed of lobster exoskeletons, although its internal structure is not known. <i>SCP-17098</i> has the ability to manipulate objects and living creatures with its claws, allowing it to steal items from unsuspecting victims.<br><br><i>SCP-17098</i>'s behavior is erratic and unpredictable; it will often wander around aimlessly for long periods of time before suddenly becoming active in pursuit of an item or person. <i>SCP-17098</i> seems to be drawn towards items that are valuable or have sentimental value, such as jewelry or photographs. Once an object has been stolen by <i>SCP-17098</i>, it will typically hide in a safe location until it can find another victim or opportunity for theft.<br><br><i>SCP-17098</i> must be contained at all times due to the potential risk posed by its thieving nature; personnel must remain vigilant when approaching any area where <i>SCP-17098</i> may be present.<br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3> <i>SCP-17098</i> is to be contained in a standard humanoid containment cell at Site-██. The cell must be equipped with motion sensors and alarms, as well as reinforced walls and locks. Any personnel entering the containment area must wear protective gear to prevent injury from <i>SCP-17098</i>'s claws.<br><br>Personnel are not allowed to interact with <i>SCP-17098</i> except under special circumstances approved by the Site Director. All items stolen by <i>SCP-17098</i> must be recovered immediately and returned to their rightful owners.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17098.1: </h3> On ██/██/20██, <i>SCP-17098</i> was observed attempting to steal a valuable painting from the ███████ Museum. The entity was successfully contained by Foundation personnel and the painting returned to its rightful owner.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17098.2: </h3> Interview with <i>SCP-17098</i><br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> Dr. ██████<br><b><br>Interviewee:</b> <i>SCP-17098</i><br><b><br>Dr. ██████:</b> Why do you steal?<br><b><br><i>SCP-17098</i>:</b> [Pauses for a moment, then speaks in a low voice] I…I don't know. It just feels like something I have to do. I don't like it, but I can't help myself.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17098.3: </h3> Experiment Log<br><b><br>Date:</b> ██/██/20██<br><b><br>Subject:</b> <i>SCP-17098</i><br><b><br>Procedure:</b> A test was conducted to observe <i>SCP-17098</i>'s behavior in the presence of valuable items. An empty room was set up with a variety of items, ranging from jewelry to money. The door was then opened and <i>SCP-17098</i> was allowed to enter the room.<br><b><br>Results:</b> <i>SCP-17098</i> immediately began searching for items of value, eventually locating and stealing a gold necklace from one corner of the room. It then quickly exited the room and attempted to flee with its prize, but was stopped by personnel before it could escape.</div>
