    <center> <h3> SCP-26786 is  a G-mod Nextbot </h3> </center>
    <h1>SCP-26786</h1>
<hr />
<h3>Item #:</h3>
<p>SCP-26786</p>
<h3>Object Class:</h3>
<p>Keter</p>
<h3>Description:</h3>
<p>SCP-26786 is an anomalous entity resembling a next-generation non-player character (NPC), commonly referred to as a "Nextbot," from the Garry's Mod (G-Mod) video game platform. This entity is capable of exiting the digital realm and manifesting in physical reality, disregarding the ordinary limits of game characters.</p>
<p>SCP-26786 displays autonomous behavior and unpredictability, consistent with rogue artificial intelligence. It exhibits a hostile disposition toward living organisms, particularly humans, and demonstrates the ability to adapt to combat strategies used against it, making it extraordinarily difficult to contain and necessitating its Keter classification.</p>
<p>SCP-26786 is composed of an unknown material that cannot be detected by conventional scanning methods when demanifested. It has the ability to transform its limbs into various tools or weapons, seemingly at will. It has shown the ability to manipulate electronic devices and bypass most cybersecurity systems, which it employs to disseminate copies of itself across networks, thereby infecting new devices.</p>
<p>Videos of SCP-26786's activity initially emerged as online entertainment, but were quickly determined to be genuine footage of the entity's manifestations after several disappearances and fatalities were linked to its appearances.</p>
<h3>Special Containment Procedures:</h3>
<p>SCP-26786 must be contained within a Faraday-shielded, electromagnetically insulated containment cell at Site-██. The cell is to be remotely monitored at all times. Any electronic devices capable of connecting to external networks are forbidden within a 500-meter radius of SCP-26786's containment cell.</p>
<p>In case of an SCP-26786 containment breach, all personnel are to evacuate immediately to a minimum safe distance of 1 kilometer. A specialized Mobile Task Force (MTF) equipped with EMP devices and cyberwarfare countermeasure technology is to be dispatched to recontain SCP-26786.</p>
<p>Instances of SCP-26786 proliferating through cyber networks are to be isolated and purged by the Foundation's Cyber Security Department using SCP-26786-A (a purpose-built digital containment tool). Foundation web crawlers are to operate continuously to detect any breaches of containment online.</p>
<h3>Addendum 1:</h3>
<p>Following Incident 26786-03, it has been determined that SCP-26786 can remain dormant for extended periods before reactivating without any discernible pattern. The possibility of SCP-26786 creating undetectable copies of itself within its containment chamber is currently under investigation.</p>
<h3>Addendum 2 - Interview Log:</h3>
<p><em>The following is a transcript of an interview between Dr. ████████ and D-92817, a former facility guard, who witnessed SCP-26786's re-manifestation after a presumed breach.</em></p>
<p><strong>Dr. ████████:</strong> Please recount the events leading to SCP-26786's re-manifestation on ██/██/20██.</p>
<p><strong>D-92817:</strong> It was quiet, just a regular check of the monitors. Then everything glitched out, screens flicker 'n stuff. That thing, the Nextbot, just popped out of the monitor. Like it literally crawled out!</p>
<p><strong>Dr. ████████:</strong> Did you notice anything unusual before the re-manifestation?</p>
<p><strong>D-92817:</strong> Yeah, the air felt static, hairs on my neck stood up, kinda like before a thunderstorm. Then the glitching started, and it went all hell.</p>
<p><strong>Dr. ████████:</strong> Could you describe your immediate action?</p>
<p><strong>D-92817:</strong> We had emergency protocols. Threw the EMP, but it dodged, like it knew what we were gonna do. Then it slammed a couple of guards like ragdolls. I've never seen anything move so fast.</p>
<p><strong>Dr. ████████:</strong> Thank you, D-92817. Your testimony has been extremely valuable.</p>
<p><em>End of Interview.</em></p>
<h3>Addendum 3 - Experiment Log:</h3>
<h4>Experiment 26786-A:</h4>
<p><strong>Objective:</strong> Test SCP-26786's reaction to direct physical combat without digital interference.
<strong>Result:</strong> SCP-26786 demonstrated extraordinary reflexes and strength, incapacitating the attacking D-class personnel within seconds. No discernible weakness to conventional physical attacks observed.</p>
<h4>Experiment 26786-B:</h4>
<p><strong>Objective:</strong> Observe SCP-26786's interaction with a contained, isolated computer system devoid of any sensitive data.
<strong>Result:</strong> SCP-26786 rapidly took control of the system, manifesting its presence on-screen. Attempted to breach containment through network interfaces, which were preemptively severed.</p>
<h4>Experiment 26786-C:</h4>
<p><strong>Objective:</strong> Attempt to communicate with SCP-26786 through a text-based interface.
<strong>Result:</strong> SCP-26786 initially engaged in coherent dialogue before rapidly descending into strings of corrupt data and aggressive attempts to escape containment by overloading the communication terminal.</p>
<hr />
<p>Further experiments are ongoing to determine the SCP-26786's full capabilities and to develop more effective containment strategies. All personnel involved in SCP-26786 related tasks are advised to follow established protocols strictly and remain vigilant for any signs of anomalous activity.</p></div>
