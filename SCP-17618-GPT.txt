<div class='justifytext'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-17618 is  the place where socks go when they vanish from the dryer</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-17618<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Euclid<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-17618</i> is a extradimensional space, accessible through any dryer with a lint trap. Upon entering <i>SCP-17618</i>, the individual finds themselves in an expansive realm filled with various types of socks.<br><br>The socks found within <i>SCP-17618</i> are always clean and appear to be freshly laundered. All socks found within <i>SCP-17618</i> have the same design and size, regardless of the type or brand of sock that was originally placed into the dryer before entry into <i>SCP-17618</i>. The exact number of socks located within <i>SCP-17618</i> is unknown as they appear to be infinite in number and constantly replenished by an unknown source.<br><br>No life forms have been observed inside <i>SCP-17618</i> other than those that enter it via a dryer lint trap; however, upon exiting through another lint trap all individuals report feeling as though they were being watched while inside this realm. No physical evidence has been gathered to support this claim thus far.<br><br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3><br><br>All dryers with lint traps are to be monitored for any unauthorized access into <i>SCP-17618</i>. Any individuals attempting to enter <i>SCP-17618</i> must be detained and questioned regarding their intentions. All personnel entering <i>SCP-17618</i> must wear protective clothing and carry a tracking device at all times in order to ensure their safe return.<br><br>In the event of an emergency, personnel may exit through any available lint trap in order to escape from <i>SCP-17618</i>.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17618.1: </h3><br><br>On ██/██/20██, a research team was sent into <i>SCP-17618</i> to investigate the source of the socks. After several hours of searching, the team eventually located what appeared to be a large machine in the center of <i>SCP-17618</i>. Upon further inspection, it was determined that this machine was responsible for creating and replenishing all socks within <i>SCP-17618</i>. The exact origin or purpose of this machine is still unknown.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17618.2: </h3> Interview with D-17618<br><b><br>Interviewed:</b> D-17618<br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> Dr. ████████<br><b><br>Dr. ████████:</b> Please state your name and occupation for the record.<br><b><br>D-17618:</b> My name is [REDACTED], and I'm a janitor at [REDACTED].<br><b><br>Dr. ████████:</b> How did you come to enter <i>SCP-17618</i>?<br><b><br>D-17618:</b> I was cleaning the dryers and found a sock stuck in the lint trap of one of them. When I tried to remove it, I suddenly found myself inside this strange place filled with socks.<br><b><br>Dr. ████████:</b> Did you notice anything unusual while inside <i>SCP-17618</i>?<br><b><br>D-17618:</b> Yeah, I felt like I was being watched the whole time. It was really creepy.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17618.3: </h3> Experiment Log<br><b><br>Experiment Log 17618.3-1:</b> On ██/██/20██, D-Class personnel were sent into <i>SCP-17618</i> with a tracking device and instructed to search for any other exits or anomalies within the realm. After approximately two hours of searching, all D-Class personnel reported feeling as though they were being watched and requested to be removed from <i>SCP-17618</i> immediately. All personnel exited through the same lint trap they had entered through without incident.</div>
