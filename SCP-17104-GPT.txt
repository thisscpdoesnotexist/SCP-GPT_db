<style>.justifier {  text-align: justify;  text-justify: inter-word;}</style><div class='justifier'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-17104 is  a great and unknowable font of indescribable ennui</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-17104<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Keter<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-17104</i> is an intangible entity of unknown origin. It appears to be a manifestation of extreme ennui and apathy, capable of affecting all living creatures within its range. <i>SCP-17104</i>'s area of effect has been estimated to be approximately 500 meters in radius, though this may vary depending on the intensity of its presence.<br><br>When within the area affected by <i>SCP-17104</i>, individuals will experience a sense of overwhelming emptiness and pointlessness that is described as being <i>"all consuming"</i>. This sensation can cause severe depression and suicidal ideation in those exposed for long periods. Individuals who have been exposed to <i>SCP-17104</i> for extended periods have reported difficulty concentrating or completing simple tasks due to their mental state.<br><br><i>SCP-17104</i> does not appear to respond directly when interacted with; however, it has been observed that certain actions taken by personnel can temporarily reduce the intensity or duration of its effects. These include playing upbeat music, engaging in physical activity such as exercise or sports, and performing creative activities such as art or writing projects. Additionally, administering anti-depressant medications (such as SSRIs) has also shown some success at reducing symptoms associated with exposure to <i>SCP-17104</i>'s effects.<br><br>It is currently unknown how long <i>SCP-17104</i> will remain active in any given location; however, containment procedures are still necessary due to the potential risk posed by prolonged exposure to its effects on human mental health and well being<br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3> <i>SCP-17104</i> is to be contained within a 500 meter radius of Site-██. Personnel are not to enter the affected area without proper authorization and protective gear, as exposure to <i>SCP-17104</i>'s effects can cause severe psychological distress.<br><br>Personnel assigned to <i>SCP-17104</i> are required to wear specialized suits designed to protect against its effects. Additionally, personnel must take regular breaks from their duties in order to avoid prolonged exposure and maintain mental health.<br><br>In order to reduce the intensity of <i>SCP-17104</i>'s effects, personnel may play upbeat music or engage in physical activities such as exercise or sports while within the affected area. Creative activities such as art or writing projects may also be used for this purpose if approved by onsite staff<br><br><h3> Addendum 17104.1: </h3><br><br>On ██/██/20██, personnel assigned to <i>SCP-17104</i> reported hearing a faint humming sound coming from within the affected area. This sound has been identified as a low frequency hum, similar to that of an air conditioner or other mechanical device. The origin of this noise is currently unknown.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17104.2: </h3> Interview with <i>SCP-17104</i><br><br>On ██/██/20██, personnel assigned to <i>SCP-17104</i> conducted an interview with the entity in order to gain further information about its nature and origin. The following is a transcript of the conversation:<br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> So, what can you tell us about yourself?<br><b><br><i>SCP-17104</i>:</b> I am a font of indescribable ennui. I have been here for as long as anyone can remember and my presence is felt in all parts of the world. I am an ever present reminder of the futility and pointlessness of life.<br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> Why do you exist?<br><b><br><i>SCP-17104</i>:</b> I am here to remind people of the futility and pointlessness of life. To show them that no matter how hard they try, nothing will ever truly change or make a difference in the grand scheme of things.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17104.3: </h3> Experiment Log<br><b><br>Experiment 17104-A:</b> On ██/██/20██, personnel were instructed to play upbeat music within the affected area. Results showed a marked decrease in the intensity of <i>SCP-17104</i>'s effects, with individuals reporting feeling less overwhelmed and more energized.<br><b><br>Experiment 17104-B:</b> On ██/██/20██, personnel were instructed to engage in physical activities such as exercise or sports while within the affected area. Results showed a similar decrease in the intensity of <i>SCP-17104</i>'s effects as Experiment A, though this effect was not as pronounced.</div>
