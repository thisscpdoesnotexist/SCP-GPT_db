<style>.justifier {  text-align: justify;  text-justify: inter-word;}</style><div class='justifier'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-24050 is  an objectively lame and uncool ghost</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-24050<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Safe<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-24050</i> is a humanoid entity resembling a teenage boy, approximately 1.7 meters tall with pale skin and dark brown hair. It wears a white T-shirt and blue jeans that are always slightly faded, as if they have been washed many times.<br><br><i>SCP-24050</i> appears to be completely intangible, unable to interact physically with its environment or other beings. Despite this, it has demonstrated the ability to speak in both English and Spanish fluently. Its voice is described as being <i>"loud but not unpleasant"</i>.<br><br>When spoken to directly, <i>SCP-24050</i> will respond in an extremely uncool manner; often making jokes at its own expense or referencing outdated pop culture references from the early 2000s such as <i>"The Matrix"</i> or <i>"Napoleon Dynamite"</i>. It also frequently expresses appreciation for activities considered uncool by modern standards such as playing board games or watching classic cartoons on VHS tapes. <br><br>In addition, when asked about its past life it will reply with various humorous anecdotes involving embarrassing situations which it claims happened while alive; however these stories are impossible to verify due their lack of detail and often implausible nature. <br><br>Despite all this, <i>SCP-24050</i> does not appear hostile towards any living creature nor does it seem particularly interested in anything aside from conversing with those who address it directly; thus far no attempts at containment have been necessary due to its lack of threat potential<br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3> <i>SCP-24050</i> is to be kept in a standard humanoid containment chamber at Site-██. It is to be monitored remotely and any personnel wishing to converse with it must receive approval from the site director first.<br><br>No attempts should be made to contain or move <i>SCP-24050</i> as it appears unable to interact physically with its environment.<br><br><h3> Addendum 24050.1: </h3> On ██/██/20██, <i>SCP-24050</i> was found in a vacant apartment building in [REDACTED], Mexico. It had been living there for an unknown amount of time and appeared to be aware of its intangible state. It is currently unknown how it came to be in this state or why it chose to inhabit the apartment building, though it did express interest when asked about the area's local folklore.<br><br><h3> Addendum 24050.2: </h3> Interview with <i>SCP-24050</i><br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> Dr. ██████<br><br><b>&ltBegin Log&gt <br>Dr. ██████:</b> Hello, <i>SCP-24050</i>. How are you today? <br><b><i>SCP-24050</i>:</b> Hey there! I'm doing alright, thanks for asking! <br><b>Dr. ██████:</b> That's good to hear. Can you tell me a bit about yourself? Where did you come from and how did you end up here? <br><b><i>SCP-24050</i>:</b> Well, I don't really remember much about my past life… but I do know that I used to be pretty uncool back then too! Haha… Yeah, it was kind of embarrassing at times but hey - at least I could still laugh about it! <br><b><br>Dr. ██████:</b> I see. Do you know why you ended up in this state? <br><b><i>SCP-24050</i>:</b> Not really, no… I guess I just kind of drifted here after death, ya know? It's not like I had any control over it or anything. <br><b>Dr. ██████:</b> Alright then. Is there anything else you'd like to tell us? <br><b><i>SCP-24050</i>:</b> Well, if anyone ever needs someone to talk to who won't judge them for being uncool, feel free to come chat with me! Haha… That's all I got for now though! &ltEnd Log&gt<br><br><h3> Addendum 24050.3: </h3> Experiment Log<br><b><br>Experiment Log 24050-1:</b> <i>SCP-24050</i> was asked a series of questions in an attempt to determine its origin. It responded with the following:<br><b><br>Q:</b> Where did you come from? <br><b>A:</b> <i>"I'm not sure, I just kind of appeared one day.
