<div class='justifytext'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-17118 is  a bag of misfortune cookies</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-17118<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Keter<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-17118</i> is a bag of fortune cookies, each containing a message that causes misfortune to the reader. The messages are written in various languages and can range from simple warnings such as <i>"beware"</i> to more complex prophecies of doom.<br><br>When an individual reads one of the messages contained within <i>SCP-17118</i>, they will experience some form of misfortune within 24 hours. This misfortune is usually related to the message that was read and can range from minor inconveniences such as car troubles or financial losses to more serious issues such as physical harm or death.<br><br>The effects caused by <i>SCP-17118</i> are not limited to those who have read its contents; individuals who come into contact with someone affected by the item may also experience similar misfortunes. Furthermore, any attempt at destroying <i>SCP-17118</i> has been unsuccessful, regardless of method used.<br><br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3><br><br><i>SCP-17118</i> is to be kept in a secure locker at Site-██. Access to <i>SCP-17118</i> is restricted to personnel with Level 3 clearance or higher. Any personnel who come into contact with the item must be monitored for 24 hours after exposure and any changes in behavior should be reported immediately.<br><br>In order to prevent further spread of misfortune, no one is allowed to read the contents of <i>SCP-17118</i> without prior approval from a Level 4 researcher. If approved, all readers must be monitored for 24 hours after reading and any changes in behavior should be reported immediately.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17118.1: </h3><br><br>On ██/██/████, a group of researchers attempted to deactivate <i>SCP-17118</i> by removing the individual fortune cookies from the bag and destroying them. This attempt was unsuccessful, as new messages appeared in place of those that had been destroyed.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17118.2: </h3> Interview with <i>SCP-17118</i><br><br>On ██/██/20██, a Level 3 researcher was given permission to interview <i>SCP-17118</i>. The following is an excerpt of the conversation that took place:<br><b><br>Researcher:</b> Why are you causing misfortune?<br><b><br><i>SCP-17118</i>:</b> I cannot help it. It is in my nature. I am nothing more than a messenger of fate, delivering warnings and prophecies to those who may be able to avert disaster.<br><b><br>Researcher:</b> But why must it be so? Why can't you just deliver good news instead?<br><b><br><i>SCP-17118</i>:</b> Because sometimes, the only way to prevent disaster is to face it head on. The warnings I give are meant to be a wake-up call, a reminder that the future is not always as it seems.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17118.3: </h3> Experiment Log<br><br>Experiment Log 17118.3<br><b>Subject:</b> D-17118-1<br><b>Procedure:</b> D-17118-1 was instructed to open one of the fortune cookies and read its contents aloud.<br><b>Results:</b> Within 24 hours, D-17118-1 experienced a series of unfortunate events, including a car accident, being robbed at gunpoint, and an allergic reaction to food that resulted in hospitalization.</div>
