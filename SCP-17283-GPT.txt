<style>.justifier {  text-align: justify;  text-justify: inter-word;}</style><div class='justifier'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-17283 is  A poetic, hopeless romantic, writer trying to promote garlic bread</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-17283<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Euclid<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-17283</i> is a humanoid entity of indeterminate gender, height and weight. It appears to be wearing an old-fashioned suit with a red bowtie, though it is not known where the clothing originated from. Its face consists of two large eyes, one blue and one green, as well as a large mouth which constantly smiles.<br><br><i>SCP-17283</i>'s primary anomalous property is its ability to produce written works of poetry about garlic bread in any language requested by its audience. These works are often romanticized descriptions about the beauty and taste of garlic bread that are highly emotive in nature. <i>SCP-17283</i> will also attempt to promote these works through various means such as advertising campaigns or public readings at local events when given the opportunity.<br><br>When asked why it produces these poems <i>SCP-17283</i> will state that it simply wishes for people to appreciate garlic bread more than they currently do, hoping that its work will bring joy and happiness into peoples' lives through their appreciation for this food item.<br><br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3><br><br><i>SCP-17283</i> is to be contained in a standard humanoid containment cell at Site-██. It is to be provided with paper and writing utensils for the production of its works, as well as access to a computer for the purpose of advertising its work.<br><br>Personnel interacting with <i>SCP-17283</i> are to remain professional and courteous at all times, avoiding any topics that may cause distress or discomfort. Any requests from <i>SCP-17283</i> must first be approved by personnel assigned to its containment before being granted.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17283.1: </h3> On ██/██/20██, <i>SCP-17283</i> was found in a small café in [REDACTED], France. It had been performing public readings of its works to patrons and staff, who were all highly impressed by the quality of the poetry. When approached by Foundation personnel it complied with requests to be taken into custody without incident.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17283.2: </h3> Interview with <i>SCP-17283</i><br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> Dr. ██████<br><b><br><i>SCP-17283</i>:</b> Hello, Doctor. How may I help you?<br><b><br>Dr. ██████:</b> We'd like to ask you a few questions about your work and why you produce it. Is that alright?<br><b><br> <i>SCP-17283</i>:</b> Of course! I'm always happy to talk about my work and its purpose. <br><b><br> Dr. ██████:</b> Could you tell us the reason why you write poems about garlic bread?<br><b><br> <i>SCP-17283</i>:</b> Well, it's something that I've been passionate about since childhood - the smell of freshly baked garlic bread is one of my fondest memories from growing up in Italy, and so I wanted to share that joy with others. I want people to appreciate garlic bread as much as I do, and so I write these poems in the hopes of inspiring them to do so.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17283.3: </h3> Experiment Log<br><b><br>Experiment 1:</b><br><b><br>Subject:</b> D-2356<br><b>Task:</b> Request a poem about garlic bread from <i>SCP-17283</i> in English.<br><b>Result:</b> <i>SCP-17283</i> produced a poem entitled <i>"The Beauty of Garlic Bread"</i> which described the smell and taste of garlic bread in great detail, as well as its ability to bring joy and happiness to those who consume it. The subject reported feeling relaxed after reading the poem, despite having no prior knowledge or interest in garlic bread before this experiment.</div>
