<div class='justifytext'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-19590 is  A common mistake made by SCP personnel, wherein the SCP class Thomiel will get accidentally written as Thaumiel</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-19590<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Thaumiel<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-19590</i> is a phenomenon wherein personnel working with the SCP Foundation will mistakenly refer to an object classified as Thomiel as Thaumiel. This mistake occurs regardless of the context in which the object is mentioned, and has been documented in both written and verbal forms of communication.<br><br>When this mistake is made, it appears that any information regarding the actual classification of the object (Thomiel) becomes lost or forgotten, leading to confusion and miscommunication among personnel. As such, it is important for all personnel to ensure they are familiar with all classifications when referring to objects within their scope of work.<br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3> Due to the nature of <i>SCP-19590</i>, containment is not necessary. Personnel are reminded to take extra care when referring to objects classified as Thomiel, and should double check their information before communicating it.<br><br>Any personnel found making this mistake will be reprimanded and given additional training on proper classifications.<br><br><h3> Addendum 19590.1: </h3> On ██/██/20██, a containment breach occurred at Site-██ due to personnel incorrectly referring to SCP-████ as Thaumiel instead of Thomiel. The breach was quickly contained and all affected personnel were reprimanded for their mistake.<br><br><h3> Addendum 19590.2: </h3> Interview with Dr. ███████<br><b><br>Interviewed:</b> Dr. ███████, Head of Containment for <i>SCP-19590</i><br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> Researcher ██████████<br><br>&ltBegin Log&gt<br><b><br>Researcher ██████████:</b> Dr. ███████, can you tell me a bit about <i>SCP-19590</i>?<br><b><br>Dr. ███████:</b> Of course. <i>SCP-19590</i> is an anomaly wherein personnel working with the Foundation will mistakenly refer to objects classified as Thomiel as Thaumiel. This mistake has been documented in both written and verbal forms of communication, and appears to be caused by confusion or lack of familiarity with the various classifications used within the Foundation's operations. As such, it is important for all personnel to ensure they are familiar with all classifications when referring to objects within their scope of work.<br><b><br>Researcher ██████████:</b> Is there any way we can prevent this mistake from occurring?<br><b><br>Dr. ███████:</b> Yes, we can take steps to ensure that personnel are aware of the various classifications used within the Foundation's operations. Additionally, extra care should be taken when referring to objects classified as Thomiel, and double-checking information before communicating it is highly recommended. Any personnel found making this mistake will be reprimanded and given additional training on proper classifications.<br><br>&ltEnd Log&gt<br><b><br>Closing Statement:</b> Dr. ███████ expressed confidence in the Foundation's ability to contain <i>SCP-19590</i> through proper education and training of personnel.<br><br><h3> Addendum 19590.3: </h3> Experiment Log<br><br>A series of experiments were conducted to determine the cause and effects of <i>SCP-19590</i>. The following is a summary of the results:<br><b><br>Experiment 1:</b> A test subject was presented with an object classified as Thomiel, and was asked to describe it. The subject mistakenly referred to it as Thaumiel, which resulted in confusion among personnel present.<br><b><br>Experiment 2:</b> A second test subject was presented with an object classified as Thomiel, and then asked to classify it correctly. Despite being given multiple chances to do so, the subject continued to refer to the object as Thaumiel. This experiment confirmed that <i>SCP-19590</i> affects all personnel regardless of their level of experience or knowledge regarding classifications within the Foundation's system.</div>
