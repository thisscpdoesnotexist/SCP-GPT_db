<style>.justifier {  text-align: justify;  text-justify: inter-word;}</style><div class='justifier'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-24025 is  is a song that makes the listener lose weight within minutes. It's safe to listen to a bit of it, but too much can cause severe malnutrition. Listening to the whole song makes you disappear entirely.</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-24025<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Euclid<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-24025</i> is a song, written in an unknown language, that causes the listener to rapidly lose weight. It has been observed that after listening to the song for approximately two minutes, subjects will begin to experience extreme hunger and dehydration. After a total of five minutes of exposure, subjects have been observed to disappear entirely with no trace left behind.<br><br>The effects of <i>SCP-24025</i> are not reversible and any attempts at resuscitation have proven unsuccessful. It is believed that the song's effects are caused by some form of extradimensional entity or force which manipulates reality in order to cause its victims' disappearance.<br><br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3><br><br><i>SCP-24025</i> is to be kept in a secure containment locker at Site-██. Access to <i>SCP-24025</i> is restricted to personnel with Level 3 clearance or higher. Personnel exposed to <i>SCP-24025</i> are to be monitored for any changes in weight and health and immediately removed from the area if any such changes occur. Any personnel who have been exposed to <i>SCP-24025</i> must undergo psychological evaluation before being allowed access again.<br><br>In order to prevent accidental exposure, all audio recordings of <i>SCP-24025</i> are prohibited from leaving Site-██ without prior approval from the O5 Council.<br><br><h3> Addendum 24025.1: </h3> On ██/██/20██, a recording of <i>SCP-24025</i> was discovered in the possession of an individual known as <i>"The Composer"</i>. The Composer claimed to have written the song himself and had been using it to kidnap people for unknown purposes. He was apprehended and is currently being held in Foundation custody.<br><br><h3> Addendum 24025.2: </h3> Interview with <i>SCP-24025</i><br><br>On ██/██/20██, a team of researchers were sent to investigate the origin of <i>SCP-24025</i>. Upon arriving at the location, they discovered an old man who claimed to be its creator. The following is a transcript of their conversation:<br><b><br>Researcher:</b> Who are you?<br><b><br>Old Man:</b> I am [REDACTED], the composer of <i>SCP-24025</i>. <br><b><br>Researcher:</b> How did you come up with this song? <br><b><br>Old Man:</b> It was given to me in a dream by something that called itself <i>"the Great Hunger"</i>. It said that if I wrote and performed this song, it would grant me great power and wealth beyond my wildest dreams. So I did as it asked and wrote the song. <br><b><br>Researcher:</b> What happened after you wrote the song? <br><b><br>Old Man:</b> I performed it in a few places, but no one seemed to take any notice of it. Then one day, someone heard it and suddenly everyone wanted to hear more. But then they all started disappearing…I realized too late that this was not what I had bargained for.<br><br><h3> Addendum 24025.3: </h3> Experiment Logs<br><b><br>Experiment Log-24025.1:</b><br><b>Subject:</b> D-Class personnel (designated D-5596)<br><b>Procedure:</b> Subject was exposed to <i>SCP-24025</i> for five minutes in a soundproof chamber.<br><b>Results:</b> After five minutes of exposure, the subject disappeared entirely with no trace left behind. The room appeared to be empty and no sign of the subject could be found.</div>
