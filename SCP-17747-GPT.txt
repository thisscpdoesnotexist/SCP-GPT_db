<div class='justifytext'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-17747 is  burger king foot lettuce</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-17747<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Keter<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-17747</i> is a fast food restaurant chain, originating in the United States, which has been infected with a highly infectious and deadly strain of bacteria. The bacteria is capable of infecting both humans and animals through direct contact with contaminated food items.<br><br>The contamination was first noticed when customers began to report an unusual taste in their burgers after eating them at the restaurant. Upon further investigation it was discovered that the source of this taste was due to <i>SCP-17747</i>'s lettuce being contaminated with a previously unknown strain of bacteria.<br><br>Once infected, victims will experience severe stomach pains, nausea, vomiting, fever and diarrhea within 12 hours after consumption. Death usually occurs within 48 hours from infection unless treated immediately with antibiotics or other medical intervention. The only known method for preventing infection is to avoid consuming any food items from <i>SCP-17747</i> restaurants altogether.<br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3> All <i>SCP-17747</i> restaurants are to be monitored and all food items are to be tested for contamination before being served. Any contaminated food items found must be immediately destroyed and the restaurant closed until further notice. All personnel entering or exiting any <i>SCP-17747</i> restaurant must wear protective gear at all times, including gloves, masks and full body suits.<br><br>Any personnel who have been exposed to the bacteria should be quarantined for 48 hours before returning to duty. Any personnel infected with the bacteria should receive immediate medical attention and treatment as soon as possible.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17747.1: </h3> On ██/██/20██, a group of researchers from the Foundation infiltrated an <i>SCP-17747</i> restaurant in [REDACTED] and discovered that the lettuce used in the burgers was infected with a previously unknown strain of bacteria.
