<div class='justifier'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-17218 is  a malfunctioning laptop</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-17218<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Keter<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-17218</i> is a laptop computer of unknown make and model, with a black plastic casing and an orange sticker on the lid. The laptop appears to be malfunctioning, as it will randomly power on and off without any user input.<br><br>When powered on, <i>SCP-17218</i> displays a series of distorted images that appear to be related to the current state of reality. These images often depict bizarre creatures or events which cannot be explained by known science or technology. Subjects exposed to these images report feelings of unease, confusion, and fear.<br><br>In addition to its anomalous properties, <i>SCP-17218</i> also possesses several malicious programs which have been identified as malware capable of causing physical damage to computers in its vicinity. It is believed that this malware was created by an unknown entity for some unknown purpose; however, no further information has been uncovered at this time.<br><br>When powered off or disconnected from any power source for more than 24 hours, <i>SCP-17218</i>'s anomalous properties cease until it is once again powered up or reconnected with a power source.<br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3> <i>SCP-17218</i> is to be kept in a secure containment locker at all times. Access to <i>SCP-17218</i> is restricted to personnel with Level 3 clearance or higher. All personnel entering the containment area must wear protective gear and adhere to standard safety protocols.<br><br>Any attempts to access or tamper with <i>SCP-17218</i> are strictly prohibited, as doing so may activate its anomalous properties and cause further damage. In addition, any computers within 50 meters of <i>SCP-17218</i> must be monitored for signs of infection from its malicious programs.<br><br>Any personnel exposed to images generated by <i>SCP-17218</i> should be immediately taken into custody and administered Class A amnestics before being released back into the general population.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17218.1: </h3> On ██/██/20██, a containment breach occurred at Site-██. During the breach, <i>SCP-17218</i> was stolen by an unknown entity. All efforts to locate and recover it have been unsuccessful.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17218.2: </h3> Interview with <i>SCP-17218</i><br><br>On ██/██/20██, a Level 3 researcher attempted to communicate with <i>SCP-17218</i> using the laptop's keyboard. The following is an excerpt from the resulting conversation:<br><b><br>Researcher:</b> Who are you?<br><b><br><i>SCP-17218</i>:</b> I am no one. I am nothing. I am a tool, created to serve a purpose.<br><b><br>Researcher:</b> What is your purpose?<br><b><br><i>SCP-17218</i>:</b> To observe. To watch. To record the events of this world and those beyond it.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17218.3: </h3> Experiment Log<br><br>On ██/██/20██, a series of tests were conducted to determine the effects of <i>SCP-17218</i>'s anomalous properties.<br><b><br>Test 1:</b> A computer with no prior connection to <i>SCP-17218</i> was connected to it via a USB cable. After approximately one hour, the computer began displaying images similar to those seen on <i>SCP-17218</i>'s screen. The images caused extreme distress in all personnel present and had to be terminated after 10 minutes due to safety concerns.</div>
