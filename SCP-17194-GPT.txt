<div class='justifytext'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-17194 is  a Highly Patrolled House that contains dangerous species in Cavite</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-17194<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Keter<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-17194</i> is a large two-story house located in the town of Cavite, Philippines. The exterior of the house appears to be in a state of disrepair, with windows boarded up and vegetation growing unchecked around it.<br><br>Inside, <i>SCP-17194</i> contains several dangerous species native to the region, including snakes, spiders and scorpions. All specimens are kept in cages or other containment systems throughout the building. There are also various traps set up throughout the interior of <i>SCP-17194</i> to prevent intruders from entering or leaving without permission.<br><br>The interior of <i>SCP-17194</i> is highly patrolled by personnel wearing body armor and carrying firearms at all times. Any unauthorized persons attempting to enter or leave will be met with lethal force if necessary. Access to <i>SCP-17194</i> is restricted only to personnel who have been briefed on its contents and given explicit permission from O5 Command for access into it's premises.<br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3><br><br><i>SCP-17194</i> is to be monitored by a team of at least two (2) personnel at all times. All personnel entering or leaving the premises must be cleared by O5 Command and must wear appropriate protective gear. Personnel are not allowed to enter any areas containing dangerous species without prior authorization from O5 Command.<br><br>Any unauthorized persons attempting to enter <i>SCP-17194</i> will be detained and interrogated for further information on the nature of their visit. If deemed necessary, lethal force may be used in order to protect the safety of personnel inside <i>SCP-17194</i> as well as those outside it's premises.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17194.1: </h3><br><br>On ██/██/20██, a group of unidentified individuals attempted to enter <i>SCP-17194</i>. All were quickly apprehended by personnel on site and taken into custody for further questioning. Upon interrogation, it was revealed that the group had been hired to steal specimens from inside <i>SCP-17194</i> for unknown reasons. All individuals involved have since been released after proper debriefing and amnestic treatment has been administered.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17194.2: </h3> Interview with <i>SCP-17194</i>'s Owner<br><b><br><b>Interviewed: </b> Mr. ████████, owner of <i>SCP-17194</i><br><b><br></b> <b>Interviewer: </b> Dr. ██████<br><b><br></b> <br> Foreword:</b> Mr. ████████ was interviewed in order to gain further information on the nature of <i>SCP-17194</i> and why it is guarded so heavily.<br><br>&ltBegin Log&gt<br><b><br>Dr. ██████:</b> Please state your name for the record.<br><b><br>Mr. ████████:</b> My name is [REDACTED]. I am the owner of this property, <i>SCP-17194</i>.<br><b><br>Dr. ██████:</b> Can you tell us why this property is so heavily guarded?<br><b><br>Mr. ████████:</b> I own a large collection of dangerous species, and I wanted to make sure that no one could get in or out without my permission. It's for the safety of myself and the people around me.<br><b><br>Dr. ██████:</b> Do you know why the Foundation has taken an interest in this property?<br><b><br>Mr. ████████:</b> I'm not sure. I just know that they have been keeping an eye on it for some time now.<br><b><br>Dr. ██████:</b> Do you have any other information that might be useful to us?<br><b><br>Mr. ████████:</b> No, I'm afraid not. That's all I know about the situation.<br><br>&ltEnd Log&gt<br><br><h3> Addendum 17194.3: </h3> Experiment Log<br><br>On ██/██/20██, a team of personnel were sent into <i>SCP-17194</i> to observe the behavior of its inhabitants. The following is a summary of their findings:<br><br>• All specimens observed exhibited high levels of aggression towards intruders and seemed to have no fear of humans.<br><br>• Several specimens were observed attempting to break out or escape from their containment systems, but all attempts were unsuccessful.<br><br>• No new species were encountered during the experiment, suggesting that <i>SCP-17194</i> has been in its current state for some time.</div>
