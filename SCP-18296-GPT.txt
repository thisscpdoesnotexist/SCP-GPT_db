<div class='justifytext'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-18296 is  aerosolized shark in a can</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-18296<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Euclid<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-18296</i> is a can of aerosolized shark, weighing approximately 300 grams. The can's label reads <i>"Shark Spray"</i> and features a cartoon image of a smiling shark with the words <i>"Spray Away Your Problems!"</i> printed underneath it.<br><br>When the contents of <i>SCP-18296</i> are released into an enclosed space, they will take on physical form as one or more sharks depending on the size and shape of the space. These sharks will then attempt to attack any living organism in the vicinity, regardless of species. The sharks created by <i>SCP-18296</i> possess enhanced strength and speed compared to their natural counterparts, making them difficult to evade or incapacitate without specialized tools or weaponry.<br><br>The effects produced by <i>SCP-18296</i> appear to be temporary; after several minutes, all instances created by its use will dissipate into nothingness without leaving any trace behind. Furthermore, no permanent damage has been observed in subjects exposed to <i>SCP-18296</i>'s effects; however psychological trauma may occur due to fear induced by its presence. <br><br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3> <i>SCP-18296</i> is to be kept in a secure locker at Site-██. Access to <i>SCP-18296</i> is restricted to personnel with Level 3 clearance or higher. Testing of <i>SCP-18296</i> must be approved by two (2) personnel with Level 4 clearance and conducted in a sealed chamber equipped with appropriate safety measures, such as gas masks and fire suppression systems.<br><br>Any instances of <i>SCP-18296</i> created during testing are to be immediately contained using standard shark containment protocols. All personnel exposed to the effects of <i>SCP-18296</i> must receive psychological evaluation before returning to duty.<br><br><h3> Addendum 18296.1: </h3> On ██/██/20██, a can of <i>SCP-18296</i> was recovered from an abandoned warehouse in [REDACTED]. The can was found among various other aerosol cans, all of which were labeled with the same <i>"Shark Spray"</i> logo. It is believed that this item was created and distributed by an unknown entity for unknown purposes.<br><br><h3> Addendum 18296.2: </h3> Interview with <i>SCP-18296</i>'s Discoverer<br><br>The following is a transcript of an interview conducted with the individual who discovered <i>SCP-18296</i>.<br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> Please state your name and occupation for the record. <br><b><br>Discoverer:</b> My name is ████ ███████, and I'm a chemist by trade. <br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> How did you come across <i>SCP-18296</i>? <br><b><br>Discoverer:</b> I was working in my lab one day when one of my assistants brought me a can that he had found in an old storage room. He said it looked interesting, so I decided to take a look at it myself. When I opened it up, I noticed that there was something inside that didn't look like a normal aerosol can. It was filled with a strange, grayish liquid that smelled like fish. <br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> What did you do next? <br><b><br>Discoverer:</b> I decided to test the contents of the can, so I sprayed it into an empty room and waited to see what would happen. A few seconds later, there were several sharks swimming around in the room! Needless to say, I was quite surprised by this development and immediately contacted your organization for assistance.<br><br><h3> Addendum 18296.3: </h3> Experiment Log<br><br><b>Experiment 18296-A <br>Date:</b> ██/██/████<br><b>Subject:</b> D-Class personnel (D-18296)<br><b>Procedure:</b> D-18296 was instructed to open <i>SCP-18296</i> in an enclosed room measuring 3m x 3m. <br><b>Results:</b> Upon opening the can, one medium sized shark manifested and immediately began attacking D-18296. The subject managed to evade the shark's attacks for approximately two minutes before being incapacitated by its bite. After three minutes, the shark dissipated without leaving any trace behind. Subject suffered minor injuries but no permanent damage was observed.</div>
