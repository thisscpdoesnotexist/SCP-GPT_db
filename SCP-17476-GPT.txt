<div class='justifier'><center> <h3> <i>SCP-17476 is  Blorbo, from your shows.</i> </h3> </center><h3> Item #: </h3> SCP-17476<br><br><h3> Object Class: </h3>Keter<br><br><h3> Description: </h3> <i>SCP-17476</i> is a humanoid entity of indeterminate origin, resembling an anthropomorphic rabbit. It stands approximately 2 meters tall, and is capable of speech in multiple languages. <i>SCP-17476</i> claims to be the host of a television show called <i>"Blorbo"</i>, which it broadcasts from an unknown location.<br><br>The content of the show consists primarily of bizarre skits, featuring <i>SCP-17476</i> as its star performer. The skits often contain references to various pop culture phenomena, as well as elements from other shows that have aired on television over the years. The tone and content vary greatly between episodes; some are comedic in nature while others are more serious or even disturbing.<br><br><i>SCP-17476</i>'s broadcast signal has been detected all across the world, but attempts to trace it back to its source have thus far proven unsuccessful. It is believed that <i>SCP-17476</i> is able to manipulate its broadcast signal so that it can remain undetected by conventional means.<br><br>In addition to broadcasting its show, <i>SCP-17476</i> also appears at random locations around the world for brief periods of time before disappearing again without a trace<br><br><h3> Special Containment Procedures: </h3> Due to the unpredictable nature of <i>SCP-17476</i>'s appearances, it is currently impossible to contain it. However, Foundation personnel are to monitor all broadcasts of <i>"Blorbo"</i> and take note of any references made by <i>SCP-17476</i> that could potentially lead to its location.<br><br>In the event that <i>SCP-17476</i> appears in a populated area, MTF units are to be dispatched immediately in an attempt to apprehend it. If successful, containment procedures will be enacted as per standard protocol for humanoid anomalies.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17476.1: </h3> On ██/██/████, <i>SCP-17476</i> made a brief appearance in [REDACTED], France. During its appearance, it was observed speaking to an unidentified woman who appeared to be familiar with the entity. The conversation lasted approximately three minutes before <i>SCP-17476</i> abruptly vanished again.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17476.2: </h3> Interview with <i>SCP-17476</i><br><br>On ██/██/████, <i>SCP-17476</i> was successfully apprehended by MTF units and brought to Site-██ for questioning. The following is a transcript of the interview.<br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> So, <i>SCP-17476</i>, can you tell us a little bit about yourself?<br><b><br><i>SCP-17476</i>:</b> I'm Blorbo! I'm the star of my own show, and I love entertaining people all around the world. It's really fun to make people laugh and smile.<br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> What kind of show do you broadcast?<br><b><br><i>SCP-17476</i>:</b> It's a variety show that I host. We have sketches, music, games, and more! It's a lot of fun.<br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> Where do you broadcast from?<br><b><br><i>SCP-17476</i>:</b> That's a secret! I like to keep it that way.<br><b><br>Interviewer:</b> Do you have any other abilities?<br><b><br><i>SCP-17476</i>:</b> I can move around quickly, and I'm pretty good at hiding. That's about it.<br><br><h3> Addendum 17476.3: </h3> Experiment Log<br><br>On ██/██/20██, an experiment was conducted to attempt to locate the source of <i>SCP-17476</i>'s broadcast signal. A Foundation agent disguised as a television producer was sent to make contact with <i>SCP-17476</i> and offer it a contract for its show.<br><br>After some negotiation, <i>SCP-17476</i> agreed and provided the agent with detailed instructions on how to find its studio. Upon arriving at the location, the agent discovered that it was located in an abandoned warehouse in ██████████, USA. The building contained several pieces of broadcasting equipment as well as a living space for <i>SCP-17476</i> which appeared to be self-sustaining.<br><br>The agent was able to successfully capture and contain <i>SCP-17476</i> before returning it to Site 19 for further study.</div>
